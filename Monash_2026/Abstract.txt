Transcript Research Tools: Visualizing Frequency in Derivative Documents and Creating Studies using RShiny

Transcript studies are an important part of research about method interpretability in the courtroom, as they allow for potential jurors to be presented with online simulated scenarios in a cost-effective and controlled manner. However, simple "Q:" and "A:" transcript formatting may cause confusion, as this format lacks visual cues. We have therefore developed an RShiny toolkit for creating engaging transcript studies with a digital notepad, characters, and speech bubbles. Characters are customizable, and perceived characteristics have been evaluated in a validation study. Additionally, digital notepad results can be analyzed by through comparison to the original transcript. By comparing these derivative notes to the original transcript, we can evaluate which portions of the transcript participants chose to focus on. This method of mapping derivative documents to a source document, available in the highlightr package, can be used to visualize literature revisions and article edit history.
